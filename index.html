Intermediate SQL - Sales Analysis

This project examines customer behavior, retention, and lifetime value using the Contoso 2024 database to uncover opportunities for increasing customer retention and maximizing revenue.

Business Questions

Customer Segmentation

Who are the most valuable customers?

Cohort Analysis

How do different customer groups generate revenue?

Retention Analysis

Which customers have not purchased recently?

Analysis Approach

1. Customer Segmentation Analysis

Customers were categorized by total lifetime value into low, mid, and high-value segments to understand revenue concentration and average value by group.

  • Segmented customers by lifetime value.
  • Compared revenue contribution and customer counts across tiers.
  • Measured average lifetime value by segment.

Key Findings

Segmentation

  • High-value customers generate the most revenue by a wide margin.
  • Average lifetime value is dominated by the high-value segment.

Cohorts

  • Customer counts and revenue weakened after the early acquisition peak.
  • Revenue per customer has steadily declined since the strongest cohorts.

Retention

  • Only a small share of customers appear to remain engaged long term.
  • The biggest weakness appears in the period immediately after the first purchase.

Strategic Recommendations

Customer Value Optimization

  • Target mid-value customers with upsell and cross-sell campaigns.
  • Introduce a VIP experience for high-value customers.
  • Build referral programs that attract similar users.

Cohort Performance Strategy

  • Tighten acquisition targeting to improve customer count.
  • Re-evaluate pricing and promotions for stronger first-order value.
  • Use earlier cohorts as benchmarks for quality.

Retention & Churn Prevention

  • Create a second-purchase program shortly after the first order.
  • Trigger personalized reminders and offers for at-risk users.
  • Prioritize retention because even small improvements multiply across cohorts.
